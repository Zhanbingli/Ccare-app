Privacy-first iOS prototype

A calmer daily loop for chronic medication routines.

ChronicCare helps users set up medication reminders, check in from Today, review reminder coverage, and connect medications with local health measurements. The current product goal is simple: support a reliable 7-day routine before adding more complexity.

What makes it different

Designed around reminder trust, not feature clutter.

Reliable reminder behavior

Scheduled reminders, snooze, follow-up logic, reminder diagnostics, refill alerts, and course-end reminders are treated as the core product system.

Low-friction daily use

Fast medication setup, camera-based OCR label scan, PRN support, streamlined logging, and a 7-day rhythm card reduce routine friction.

Local-first by default

Data stays on device by default. OCR suggestions are editable candidates, and the app does not provide diagnosis or medical advice.

Core capabilities

Built around the workflows that decide whether users return.

  • Adaptive reminders with scheduled notifications, snooze, and follow-up logic
  • Medication add/edit flows with PRN logic, inventory, course duration, and camera OCR suggestions
  • Measurement logging for blood pressure, glucose, weight, and heart rate
  • Medication detail views with reminder explanation and related measurement trends
  • Emergency card and caregiver support flows
  • Backup, restore, PDF export, and HealthKit helpers

Current focus

Proving the 7-day routine

The current work is focused on a narrow validation path: setup friction, notification reliability, daily check-in clarity, OCR correction, and first-week retention.

Privacy posture

Local-first, with clear medical boundaries

Data is stored locally by default. OCR results must be confirmed by the user. The app is for self-management support and does not provide medical advice.

Alpha limitations

Transparent about what still needs validation.

  • Real-device notification regression is still required before any App Store release.
  • OCR can misread medication labels and is only used to reduce typing.
  • Caregiver support is a product prototype, not a clinical escalation system.
  • The app is not a medical device and does not provide medical advice.

Next milestone

Small real-user trial

The next meaningful milestone is a 5-10 person, 7-day test focused on setup success, reminder trust, daily check-ins, and whether users return without being prompted by the developer.
